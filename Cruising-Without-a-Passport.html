Cruising Without A Passport

  • March 01, 2018
  • TravelCruising

You may have thought it was impossible, but after a crazy 24 hours, I can confirm that cruising without a passport is in fact, permissible under the right circumstances.

To make a long story short, we had one business day to renew a passport (the Friday before a Monday holiday, to make matters even more urgent) after finding out late Thursday night that our travel companion’s passport had expired in February. The kicker was that he was flying into New York on Friday morning, so he would only be in person in New York for about 4 business hours, and a passport renewal needs the passport holder to be present, of course. Hopefully no one has to go through the panic that we went through, but if anything, going through it ourselves can reassure you that if you are in this situation, there is still hope!

The Situation:

  • International travel in less than 24 hours/1 business day
  • Expired Passport
  • Arrival in Departure city at 1PM

The Solution:

  • New York Passport Agency (Greater New York Federal Building, 376 Hudson Street, New York, NY 10014-3621) Open 8AM-6PM Monday-Friday
  • Proof of international travel
  • Passport photos
  • All required supporting documents for a passport renewal
  • A major Credit Card, Debit/Check card (not ATM cards), Check, Bank Draft, Cash (Exact Change), or Money Order to cover the application and expedited fees

Luckily for us, the passport office was not extremely crowded, which was surprising for the Friday before a holiday. Customers who have travel dates within 2 weeks are directed to the expedited line, where no appointment is necessary. You will need to bring proof of travel within 2 weeks (an itinerary or travel confirmation), the necessary paperwork for renewing a passport (including 2 passport photos), and the fee for renewing the passport ($110 plus $60 for expediting the request- trust me, it’s worth it!). It was crazy listening to all the reasons that brought people to the passport office so soon before their travel dates. Many people were surrounded by luggage, hoping to leave for the airport to catch flights that night, and there was even a classic “my dog ate my passport” situation. Armed with the necessary documents, you will be given an appointment to renew your passport. Waiting in line is probably the longest wait you will have if your travel is within 2 weeks, and if you get an appointment before 2:30PM (and have all the right documents), they should be able to process your passport that day. We got an appointment at around 2:30, and were told to come back before 6PM to pick up the new passport. At 6PM there were still a huge amount of people waiting for their passports, but eventually we received the new passport, much to our relief.


After all of the stress that we went through trying to get the passport renewed with such short notice, I did a quick google search and found out that for closed loop cruises (leaving and arriving from the same US port), a passport is actually not necessary if you bring a certified birth certificate and a government-issued driver’s license. We learned the hard way, but if someone else learns from our mistake, I think it was worth it. Happy traveling!
