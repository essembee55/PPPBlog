22 Hours In Chengdu, China

  • March 15, 2018
  • Travel

We got our 10-year Chinese visas one year ago, so when we found really cheap flights back to New York via Chengdu, China from Hong Kong, we jumped at the opportunity to spend a 22 hour layover in China. Having barely heard of Chengdu, we were pleasantly surprised to discover that there is a wealth of amazing things to do in Chengdu and the surrounding area, and our 22 hours allowed us to barely scratch the surface.

We narrowed down the places that we wanted to see with our limited time to: Chengdu Panda Base, Dujiangyan Irrigation System and old street, and Mount Qingcheng. Because of our limited time, Jeremy contacted a tour guide, Haba, to show us around. We chose Haba because he wanted to show us less touristy places, which we love!

Haba picked us up in his roomy van at 7:30 AM after our delicious breakfast at our hotel. He was kind enough to offer to drive us to the airport after our trip, so we loaded all of our stuff into the van. Our first stop was to the famous Chengdu Panda Base. We were advised by Haba to get to the Panda Base as early as possible, as this is when the pandas are most active. Tourists arriving at 10am or later may be disappointed to only see sleeping pandas. We were delighted to see pandas of all ages eating, playing, climbing, and of course sleeping. The Panda Base is one of Haba’s favorite places and he knows it like the back of his hand. We saved so much time by being with him because he knew exactly where to go. If you are on a layover in Chengdu without a tour guide, I would recommend setting aside a half day to explore the Panda Base because the place is HUGE. The Chengdu Panda Base is also home to several Red Pandas which were very active as well.

From the Panda Base, Haba drove us 1.5 hours to the Dujiangyan area, an UNESCO World Heritage Site. This is the home to one of the world’s oldest irrigation systems, which is still in use today. Before our hike up the mountain and around the irrigation system, we stopped for a lunch of the most delicious dagger noodles that I have ever tasted. Chengdu is located in Sichuan, an area famous for its spices, and the flavor of the noodles was divine.

Our hike around Dujiangyan lasted about 3 hours, so be prepared with water and a light snack. I was able to do the hike in half-inch heels, so it’s not an intense hike, just long (there is also the option to pay to use the escalator that will take you down the mountain). Our first stop was to the pagoda watchtower that was recently rebuilt after it was damaged by an earthquake in 2008. We then hiked down the mountain and made stops at lovely bridges and temples along the way. There is a beautiful Bonsai garden that can be found after crossing the bridge.

On our way back to the airport, we stopped at Mount Qingcheng, which was gorgeous. The mountain air was so fresh, and we were surrounded by beautiful fields of yellow rapeseed flowers. I was initially hesitant about a 22 hour layover, but Haba and Chengdu changed my mind, and I still have a giant smile on my face thinking about how amazing our short time in Chengdu was. Thank you Haba, and thank you Chengdu!
