How To Survive Food Poisoning In Thailand

  • March 01, 2018
  • Thailand

As pretty experienced travelers with well-adjusted (we thought) stomachs, Jeremy and I thought we were invincible to food poisoning... until we weren’t.

The horror hit us both like a train in the middle of the night, and we spent 3 days desperately trying to get over it. Luckily, we were staying in a really nice hotel with fantastic air conditioning, a buffet breakfast, and a pool to cool off in. If at all possible, I would not recommend staying in a hostel while subdued with food poisoning- when you have to go to the bathroom every 5 minutes and climb over people and out of a bunk bed each time, it makes for a miserable experience for everyone involved.

I’m still not sure what caused the food poisoning, but ever since this is what I try to do to stay food poisoning free:

  • Be really careful about ice (make sure there are holes in the middle of your ice cubes which indicates that it’s probably clean)
  • Inspect prep areas (if possible) of people who are serving raw food like sushi and fruit
  • Watch out for smoothies that are made using sketchy water (this is high on my suspect list)
  • Drink bottled water if possible. If you’re really crazy/careful, brush your teeth with bottled water, but I don’t think that’s necessary in Thailand
  • Wash your hands!!!!! (Duh)

In the end, you can’t control everything that you eat, so don’t make yourself miserable trying to avoid food poisoning, just make reasonable choices and you should be ok!
That said, if it’s already too late, here is what we did to combat our bout of food poisoning:


Unlike the United States, pharmacists in Thailand can give out most medications without a prescription from a doctor. I was able to get an inhaler to treat a cough that I had, and so the only thing preventing us from seeing a pharmacist sooner was simply being unable to get out of bed. The pharmacist that we visited was excellent, and luckily spoke excellent English. He prescribed two different pills to help us get over our trouble with going to the bathroom, and also gave us several packets of rehydration powder. The rehydration powder made water taste like Gatorade, so we bought a couple extra to keep for emergencies. Overall, the worst of the sickness was about 2 days, and we were bedridden for one day. Before we got our medicine, drinking lots of water and sticking to a BRAT diet helped to make us a little more comfortable. If possible, get to a pharmacist ASAP for relief, and stay hydrated!
