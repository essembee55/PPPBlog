An Updated Guide To The Taipei 101's Starbucks

  • March 04, 2018
  • TaiwanFood

AKA the highest Starbucks in the world

The first time I ever visited this not-so-secret-anymore Starbucks location was with my aunt, and she made all of the arrangements for us. On this trip to Taipei, I wanted to take Jeremy, and so it was up to me to make all of the reservations on my own. I do have a slight advantage because my Chinese is good enough to make reservations, but I think the employees do speak enough English that if you are patient, you will be totally fine.

1. The Reservation: Call at least a day ahead. I didn’t know which specific day would be best for us to go, so i didn’t book way ahead of time. I got lucky, and when I called one day in advance, there was availability at 3 different times the next day. When I saw their reservation list, mine was at the very bottom, so clearly I was the last one to call! For all you planners out there, it’s best to call as early as possible, as your entrance number to the restaurant is dictated by the order that the reservations were made. Also make sure to write down your reservation number, as they will ask for it when you check in.

2. The Day Of: arrive at the lobby of the office building entrance of Taipei 101 no later than 5 minutes before your reservation time. From the food court (enter at Din Tai Fung from the MRT) take the escalator up one floor. I asked the information counter worker for directions just to be sure. If you are the first one there, look for a sign with instructions for Starbucks patrons. Most likely though, there will already be a line, and it’s easy to spot. A Starbucks worker will come down to fetch you. They will ask for your reservation number, and will give you a ticket with your order number on it. This dictates the order that you will be able to choose your seats.

3. Inside Starbucks: Once you exit the elevator, you will be allowed to enter in the order on your ticket that was given downstairs. We were the last ones to enter, but we still got great seats. We went for a window seat because they have the best view, but there are many other options that are just as nice. You are required to spend at least $200NTD per person, which is perfect for 2 drinks and a pastry or two for 2 people (much cheaper than a ticket to the observatory, and just as good of a view)
