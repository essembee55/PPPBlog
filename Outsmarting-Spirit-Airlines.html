9 Ways To Outsmart Spirit Airlines

  • February 17, 2018
  • Travel Smarter

If you’ve never flown Spirit Airlines, the airline infamous for cheap flights and countless fees, there are some things to know. Frustratingly, my mother recently violated 2 of my below rules while preparing for her flight this weekend, so I thought it was the perfect time to share my knowledge so that no one else makes the same mistakes!

Spirit Airlines is a budget airline, but that does not make it a bad airline. However, what that means is you need to be smart to play the game: you need to know how to avoid the fees and not be picky. This will get you to your destination normally at a fraction of the cost, but it may not be a luxury experience, as the bare fare Spirit experience does not include free bags, snacks, in-flight entertainment and nice legroom. If you’re like us and prefer to spend your money on experiences on land versus in-flight comfort, this post is for you.

1. Do not Check Bags or Carry on a large bag
Most airlines these days charge for baggage- tip of my cap to Southwest for baggage fee-less travel. Spirit airlines charges baggage fees for BOTH carry on and checked bags. Sometimes this can be as much as 49 dollars per bag per way with a maximum weight of 40 pounds- 10 pounds lighter than the standard at other airports. It’s 100 dollars if you go over that. It’s best to purchase early as the price goes up at both the airport and/or 24 hours before your flight.

2. Maximize your Personal Item
One way around the baggage fee is to pack light and into one bag deemed the “personal item”. I have been doing this for years, as the personal item is far larger than you may expect. With a size of 18 inch by 14 inch by 8 inch it comes out to the size of a small duffle bag- more than fine for a nice weekend trip. Sonia has even managed to pack a duffle bag for a week-long cruise in a Spirit-sized “personal item”. Therefore, if you can manage it, just pack into one bag whether it be a duffle-bag, backpack, etc. Ladies- put your purses inside the bag!

3. Print your boarding pass at home
Yep- Spirit charges 10 dollars for printing your boarding pass at the airport. An easy way to avoid this is to merely print your boarding pass at home with your printer or even Fedex or Staples for 12 cents.

4. Book on Spirit.com
Spirit fares are $10 more expensive when you book over the phone or via third-party websites such as Orbitz. Booking on those sites also means you're more likely to get hit by an unexpected fee because you'll miss the various disclaimers about extra charges that Spirit.com customers receive. I normally start on skyscanner.com and then before I book I always check the direct airline to see if it’s cheaper, which in Spirit’s case it is.

5. Do not get Assigned Seats and Do not check in Online 24 hours before
Spirit charges for assigned seat fees, although normally if you are on the same reservation then you will be seated together. While sometimes they let you move, most Spirit flights are filled due to them trying to reduce overhead so what I normally do is wait until 8 hours before my flight to check in online. My theory is that for flights that are completely full they normally give out the middle seats first. Therefore, the longer you wait to check in online, the better chance you have of getting the coveted aisle or window seat for no additional charge.

6. Bring your own food and drinks
Nearly everything on board Spirit is for sale, including water for $3. Bring an empty water bottle through security and fill it at an airport water fountain. If you forget a bottle, order a glass of ice— which is the one free thing on board— and wait for it to melt.

7. Buy your tickets at the airport
This is a trick only the most frugal travelers know. Spirit charges a "passenger-usage fee" on every booking made online—typically $17 each way. (Spirit made $150 million from this fee alone last year.) BUT there is no fee if you book your ticket at the airport—like travelers did decades ago. Typical savings: $34 per round-trip ticket if you have already scouted out the deal.

8. Join the 9 dollar fare club
While Spirit no longer offers 9 dollar fares, this membership can sometimes actually cost you less than getting the standard fare. It is common to see that if you enroll it will cost you less than the 59 dollar fee for enrolling. Just be sure to cancel the yearly membership by written notice.

9. Look at the connection flight routes
Sometimes it is the flights that are going farther that end up being cheaper than going just to your destination, especially if you are going to a major city or hub.
